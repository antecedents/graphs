Augmenting Decisions

          

          


            
            


            

            		
          

          

Herein, the models of an A&E (accident & emergency) centre predict its trend and seasonal values.  The band estimates are estimates that include residue noise bands; study the fundamental, mathematical briefs, and project details pages for more details.  In brief, the attendance numbers of each A&E centre will include random counts due to irregular, rare catastrophic, etc., events.  It is foolhardy to predict this noise.  An option → assuming an attendance numbers series is akin to

            $$\mathbf{y} = T + S + R$$

            wherein $\mathbf{y}$ is the series of attendance numbers, $T\;$ denotes the trend component, $S\;$ the seasonal component, and $R\;$ the residue, noise.  Then we can use the quantile bands of $R\;$ to propose band estimates of attendance numbers.



            Consequently, experts at an emergency centre have the wherewithal to decide which band of prediction estimates should guide their forward and contingency plans.  A centre might not opt for the largest band due to cost considerations, and because the likelihood that a week's attendance numbers will fall within the band is miniscule.
