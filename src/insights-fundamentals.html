If a time series signal $\mathbf{y}$ has plausible seasonal $S$ & trend-cycle $T$ components, predicting via components is an option1. Especially if the mechanics of the residue, i.e., $R = \mathbf{y} - S - T$, are unknown; the underlying issue might be both aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty2. Let $\mathbf{y}$ be an institution's series of weekly accident & emergency attendances, then considering $$\mathbf{y} = S + T + R$$ wherein $S$, $T$, & $R$ are the season, trend, & residue components of a series, respectively, this project
  • Predicts future attendance trends via Bayesian machine learning.
  • Predicts future season patterns via a naive Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average models.
  • Estimates attendance numbers bands.
